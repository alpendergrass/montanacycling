2001 Rocky Mountain Roubaix

USCF and Collegiate Road Race

Saturday, April 14th

Collegiate Hill Climb

Sunday, April 15th

Promoted by: Team Stampede / Five Valley Velo

And University of Montana Cycling Team

Held under USAC permit

Saturday April, 14th 2001 will mark the 20th annual RMR. This classic cycling event is modeled after the famous Paris Roubaix. This 35-60 mile road race has significant sections of unpaved roads. Racers are encouraged to USE HEAVY WHEELS AND TIRES. Flats and out-of-true rims are not uncommon. Support will be "wheel-in, wheel-out" only.

On Sunday, April 15th there will be a collegiate only hill climb. The hill climb course is entirely paved. It is approximately 3.5 miles long. This event is a Montana Cup Points Series event.

Homestays are available. Email or call Jeff Crouch or Corbin Schwanke for arrangements to be made.

All USCF rules apply.

Helmets must be worn during race and warm-up.

All road courses are open to traffic. Racers have the use of the right lane only. Yellow line violations will result in disqualification. Promoter reserves the right to combine categories. Racers should realize this racecourse contains rough, unpaved sections of road. Licenses will be available on race day. Non-licensed racers are required to purchase a one-day racing license. Race is neutral during train crossings.
