Montana Bicycle Racing Association

Board Of Director’s Meeting Minutes

August 2, 2003

 

The initial meeting of the Montana Bicycle Racing Association Board of Directors was called to order at 3:20 pm at the Sunshine Station, Philipsburg, Montana.

 

Present at the meeting were John Weyhrich, Don Russell, Tim Bechtold (Team Stampede/Five Valley Velo); Scott Herzig (Montana Velo); Dave Landstrom (Flathead); Jason Mohr (Northern Rockies); Byron Deford (Great Divide).

 

1.                  The first order of business was consideration of the proposed bylaws of MBRA. All present approved the bylaws and all subsequent business was transacted according to the bylaws.

 

2.                  The Board then nominated and elected officers of MBRA.

 

Byron Deford nominated John Weyhrich for President. No other nominations were received. Weyhrich was elected as the founding President of MBRA.

 

Dave Landstrom nominated Steve Willis for Vice-President. No other nominations were received. Willis was elected as the founding Vice-President of MBRA.

 

Deford nominated Scott Herzig for Secretary. No other nominations were received. Herzig was elected as the founding Secretary of MBRA.

 

Jason Mohr nominated Deford for Chief Financial Officer. No other nominations were received. Deford was elected as the founding CFO of MBRA.

 

3.                  Designation of duties.

 

Deford moved to designate the duties of CFO to Steve Dolberg. Weyhrich seconded. Without further discussion the board voted unanimously to designate the duties of CFO to Steve Dolberg until further notice.

 

Herzig moved to designate the clerical and secretarial duties of the Secretary to Tim Bechtold. Weyhrich seconded. Without further discussion the board voted unanimously to designate the clerical and secretarial duties of the Secretary to Tim Bechtold until further notice.

 

Weyhrich moved to designate himself as Scheduling and Events coordinator, in conjunction with Tim Bechtold. Mohr seconded. Without further discussion the board voted unanimously to designate Weyhrich and Bechtold as Scheduling and Events coordinators for MBRA.

 

Weyhrich moved to designate Rob Whitacre as both Official’s Coordinator and Upgrades Coordinator. Deford suggested that a person more familiar with Montana racers and races might be better suited to act as Upgrades Coordinator than Whitacre. Landstrom noted that this was an area of concern for racers. Bechtold suggested that a person known and respected by racers and who is familiar with the racers would be a better fit than Whitacre as Upgrades Coordinator. Don Russell noted that shouldering both tasks was an onerous burden and offered to act as Upgrades Coordinator. Weyhrich amended his motion to designate Rob Whitacre as Official’s Coordinator and Don Russell as Upgrades Coordinator. Deford seconded. Without further discussion the board voted unanimously to designate Whitacre as Official’s Coordinator and Russell as Upgrades Coordinator.

 

4.                  2003 Schedule

 

The board discussed the cyclocross schedule for the fall.  Landstrom noted that Flathead would like to host the State Championship race on November 8 or 9, and so moved. Weyhrich seconded, and all approved. Mohr suggested that cross races be on Saturdays if possible, rather than Sundays, noting that fewer schedule conflicts arise on Saturdays for many racers. Weyhrich suggested that Montana Velo and Great Divide host their cross races back-to-back on a Saturday and Sunday, since few racers are willing to travel across the state for a one-hour race, having two races in the same town on the same weekend might entice more racers. Weyhrich also suggested that Northern Rockies and Five Valley Velo could do the same on one weekend in Missoula as well, so racers could get more racing with less travel time. Bechtold stated that he and Mike Beltz were organizing a weekday cross series for Northern Rockies and Five Valley Velo in Missoula that would run six weeks from mid-September until late October, probably at Fort Missoula. Weyhrich suggested that the series be on Wednesday nights so racers could be ready for weekend racing. Bechtold stated that he would post a tentative schedule for the 2003 cross races on the listserv and the final schedule could be worked out in the next week or so via email. All agreed.

 

5.                  2004 Schedule

 

Landstrom stated that Flathead wanted to move its race to sometime in May or June. Bechtold suggested that MBRA consider moving all road races so the season ends in mid-July, perhaps at the time of the Cascade Classic. Herzig noted that MBRA needs to try a different schedule, since there is such a dramatic drop in racer numbers for July and August races. Deford suggested that MBRA set a time for the state championship race and have that be the end of the season, perhaps at the end of June or early in July, so that Montana racers have two big events to train for, first the Ecology Center race and then the state championship race at least four or five weeks later. Given that the Ecology race is on Memorial Day weekend, the end of June would be far enough removed to allow another training goal. Mohr offered that the end of June is the traditional time for championship races, and it would work well to have Montana’s championship race then. Weyhrich pointed out that MBRA needs to work its schedule around some of the large regional races so that Montana racers can travel to these races, mostly in Washington and Oregon. Bechtold informed the board that Five Valley Velo was interested in truncating its spring training series to one race and having the remaining weekends in March available for Cup Series races, either for Five Valley Velo races or other races. Deford commented that the training series races are important for attracting new people to racing and we should not change them all to “real” races. There was general agreement that the first flat race at Clinton should be a training race, but the later races could be Cup Series races. Weyhrich noted that he was interested in developing new venues if open dates were available for Cup Series races. All agreed not to make a firm decision on the 2004 schedule, but to discuss it further within the member clubs. Bechtold offered to draft a proposed schedule and post it for comment and review.

 

6.                  Montana Cup Series

 

All present expressed an interest in enhancing the Montana Cup Series. Herzig suggested that the series jersey be a leader’s jersey that the current leader wears, rather than the past year’s winner. All agreed that this was good idea, and Weyhrich moved for approval to purchase three jerseys for 2004, estimating that the jerseys will be about $70-80 each. Deford seconded. All approved. The jerseys would only be for Men Cat 1,2 and W Cat 1,2,3, though other cats will have series competition.

 

The board approved the following for the Montana Cup Series:

1.      There will be separate scoring for the following eight classes: Men 1,2; Men 3; Men 4,5; Masters 35+; Masters 45+; Masters 55+; Women 1,2,3; Women 4.

2.      MBRA will issue two copies of the same number to each racer at the start of the season or upon that racer’s first series race. The racer will pay $1 for the numbers. The racer will retain the number for the remainder of season. If the racer loses or mangles one copy of the number, the racer can use the second one. If the racer loses both, then the racer will have to pay $5 to have a replacement number issued.

3.      Numbers will be issued in unique groupings for each class. For example, all Men 1,2 racers will have numbers between 1 and 99. All Women 1,2,3 racers will have numbers between 101 and 199, etc.

4.      Each club can still create class groupings as it chooses for its race and award prizes according to its own groupings, but Cup Series points will be derived according to the Cup Series classes. For example, a race may have a Men 1,2,3 class, a Men 4,5 class, a Masters 35+ class, and a Women’s class. For Cup Series rankings, the Cat 3 men would be awarded points according to their finish relative to other Cat 3 men in the race. Similarly, masters who are really 55+ would be awarded points according to their finish relative to other 55+ racers, etc.

5.      MBRA will create and maintain a series database that will be updated after each race and placed on the website so racers can see current Cup Series standings.

6.      In addition to the jerseys for top Men 1,2 and Women 1,2,3 riders, other class winners will receive medals or trophies or some similar token. Furthermore, there will be cash prizes for top finishers in each class. The cash prizes for each class in the series will go at least three deep and perhaps deeper depending on the size of the classes. 

7.      Keith Carparelli, Tim Bechtold, and Steve Dolberg will administer the Cup Series in 2004.

 

7.                  Finances

 

Weyhrich reported that MBRA has received $995 to date from USCF in license payments. MBRA receives $5 from every license fee that a Montanan pays to USCF. Board members agreed that as a general policy the President of MBRA could spend up to $100 for managerial or clerical expenses at his discretion after informing the rest of the board via email, but that expenses of more than $100 need the approval of a majority of the board. Under the bylaws, email approval is OK, so the President can approve of an expense as soon as a majority of the designated directors email that the expense is OK.

 

Bechtold suggested that the board consider a $1 per racer per race surcharge to go to MBRA to support things like the Cup Series prizes and other expenses that may arise. Deford stated that a $1 per racer is often the difference between breaking even in a race, and this could be a burden for promoters. Herzig suggested that promoters should just increase their entry fee by a dollar. Deford related the hassle of making change if an entry fee were raised to $21 from $20. Mohr and Herzig both noted that racers would not balk at the dollar increase if they knew the money was going to fund the overall series prize money. Landstrom suggested that such a surcharge be devoted to fund the Montana Series Cup and potentially other things like junior development. Mohr moved that MBRA impose a $1/racer/race surcharge. Landstrom seconded. All approved without further discussion.

 

8.                  Equipment

 

The board agreed without entertaining a motion that MBRA should not attempt at this time to purchase any equipment that could be used at all races, such as timing equipment, safety equipment, finish line cameras, etc.

 

9.                  Officials

 

Bechtold suggested that each club be obligated to supply an official to a different club’s race so that we can avoid the appearance of impropriety and comply with the rules requiring non-host club judges. Russell expressed concern that inexperienced officials should not be tossed into a race situation without some guidance and support. Mohr commented that there needed to be flexibility for officials to makes changes as their time commitments developed. Weyhrich commented that both these concerns should be easy to avoid with some advance scheduling and flexibility that allows clubs and officials to make changes as situations arise. Herzig noted that smaller clubs might have difficulty providing an official. Russell stated that he would be conducting an official’s clinic in February so people would have the chance to upgrade or become officials then. Herzig then moved, Weyhrich seconded, that each club provide an official for at least one other club’s race per year. The board approved this with no further discussion.

 

10.              Juniors

 

Bechtold suggested that the board consider devoting funds to junior development and racer recruitment. Herzig noted that Geoff Proctor had volunteered to coordinate or be involved in junior development, but that it would cost some money for expenses. Each board member offered some idea of what junior development might entail and what it might cost, and in the end decided that the most prudent path would be to seek proposals from people interested in working on junior development that includes both an outline of a proposed program and proposed costs. Deford moved that MBRA put out a request for such proposals and the board could review them and decide what to do at a later date. Landstrom seconded. All were in favor.

 

11.              Junior Entry Fees

 

Deford moved, Herzig seconded, that junior entry fees in all MBRA Cup Series races be a maximum of $5. Deford actually wanted the entry fees for juniors to be zero dollars, but because of USCF and MBRA fees, $5 seemed like a reasonable maximum. All approved. So the entry fee for juniors is now set at a maximum of $5 for all MBRA Cup Series races.

 

12.              Scheduling Conflicts

 

Deford wondered if there were a way to make sure that more than one bicycle event is not scheduled for the same day in Montana, including rides such as TSRV and RATPOD, mountain bike races, and road races. Deford noted specifically the conflict between the Bozeman and Dillon mountain bike races, which were held on the same day. Mohr wondered what sort of influence MBRA could have on NORBA events and NORBA scheduling. Weyhrich noted, and all agreed that we should work cooperatively to try to avoid schedule conflicts, but that the free market prevails and MBRA cannot stop someone from holding an event.

 

That concluded the items of discussion, and Herzig moved, Weyhrich seconded, to adjourn the meeting. All in favor. Meeting adjourned at 4:45 pm.
