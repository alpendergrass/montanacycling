2002 Rocky Mountain Roubaix

USCF and Collegiate Road Race

Saturday, March 30

Collegiate Hill Climb and Criterium

Sunday, March 31

Promoted by: Team Stampede / Five Valley Velo

And University of Montana Cycling Team

Held under USAC permit

Saturday March 30, 2002 will mark the 21st annual Rocky Mountain Roubaix. This classic cycling event is modeled after the famous Paris Roubaix. This 35-50 mile road race has significant sections of unpaved roads. Racers are encouraged to USE HEAVY WHEELS AND TIRES. Flats and out-of-true rims are not uncommon. Support will be "wheel-in, wheel-out" only. This event is a Montana Cup Points Series event.

On Sunday, March 31 there will be a collegiate only uphill individual time trial and criterium. The time trial course and criterium course are entirely paved. The time trial is approximately 3.5 miles long. The criterium course is an 8 corner 1.1 mile circuit with some sharp corners and bumps.

Homestays are available. Email or call Jeff Crouch or Corbin Schwanke for arrangements to be made.

All USCF rules apply.

Helmets must be worn during race and warm-up.

The road race and time trial courses are open to traffic. Racers have the use of the right lane only. Yellow line violations will result in disqualification. Promoter reserves the right to combine categories. Racers should realize this racecourse contains rough, unpaved sections of road. Licenses will be available on race day. Non-licensed racers are required to purchase a one-day racing license. Race is neutral during train crossings.
