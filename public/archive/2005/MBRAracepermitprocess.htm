Subject: MBRA race permit process 

 

Date: Wed, 24 Mar 2004

 

From: Timothy Bechtold

 

 

There have been a few discrepancies in the bike race permitting process that the Montana Dept Transportation and Highway Patrol and USA Cycling have called me about this month, so here is a reminder of what steps have to be taken to permit a bike race in Montana:    

 

  1. MBRA has a scheduling meeting to cooperatively work out what the race schedule for the year will be. It is not a first-come, first-served race to send in a permit. Under MBRA's contract with USAC, MBRA decides what races will be the USAC-permitted events in Montana. The MBRA board is the entity responsible for deciding the schedule for the season. I enforce the schedule by only approving events on the schedule. 

 

  1. Once the schedule is set, the MDT and MHP have reminded me that a race promoter should contact her MDT District Administrator and apply for a MDT permit to use the roadway for a bike race. 

 

  1. Once the promoter has the MDT permit in hand, she should fill out a USAC permit application, write a check to USAC for the proper amount, and send the permit application to me, with the check, a copy of the MDT permit, and a safety plan for the race. Address is below. 

 

  1. Next the promoter should contact her local MHP and county sheriff and inform them of the race, preferably in writing.  

 

  1. As USAC has reminded me, I am responsible for reviewing the permit application and safety plan, signing on behalf of MBRA, and sending the permit application to USAC.

 

  1. USAC will cash the check and will send a copy of the permit to the promoter and to me once approved.     A reminder: new this year with USAC is the additional insurance required of any automobile involved in the race, such as a wheel car or official's car. If you plan to have automobile support, be sure to include this very expensive item.     If anyone has questions about this procedure, please do not hesitate to contact me. We have had a much more informal procedure in the past, but our past procedure does not conform to current requirements of MDT, MHP, and USAC.
