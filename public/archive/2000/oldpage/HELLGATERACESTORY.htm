HELLGATE DUATHLON RACE STORY

Missoula Hellgate Duathlon

 

Cool temperatures, a wet course and a smattering of rain were not enough to

stop Georgia Gould from winning the individual women’s category and besting

the women’s course record by two minutes at Saturday’s seventh annual Five

Valley Velo / Team Stampede Hellgate Duathlon.

"It didn’t feel fast doing it," Gould said. "It felt bad."

The race course, at Kreis Pond in the Ninemile Ranger District West of

Missoula, started with a 4k run. The first half took runners on a fast

sprint down through the woods only to then make them struggle right back up.

After a 15k mountain bike leg, the competitors had a second go on the same

run course for another 4k.

"That second climb was never ending," Gould said. A newcomer to Missoula

from Ketchum, Idaho, she has just started racing and this was her first

duathlon.

For UM student Emily Sandall, who took second in the women’s category, this

duathlon was also a first.

"It was an exciting race," Sandall said. "The organization was really

good."

In the men’s category, Hellgate High Staffer Mike Birnbaum led pretty much

from beginning to end.

"I felt really strong," Birnbaum said. "It was pretty exciting on the

bike."

Jacob Hartsoch (TS5VV-Msla), who placed second overall, stayed with Birnbaum

during the bike leg.

"I felt pretty good on the bike," Hartsoch said. "But still it was all I

could do to stay with him. I was thinking ‘Who is this guy?’"

Coming into the second transition, Hartsoch was only ten seconds behind

Birnbaum. "You can kinda fake it on the first run," Hartsoch said of his

own performance. But on the second run he saw his chance for a victory

evaporate when he lost close to a minute on the lead, allowing Mike Birnbaum

to run away with a win.
