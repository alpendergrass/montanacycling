Fact Sheet - District Championships Criterium Proposal



What: A criterium is a multi-lap bicycle racing event held on a closed circuit, usually around a few city blocks. The race will include three races for differing categories of riders, with race lengths varying from 40 minutes to 60 minutes. The criterium format is designed to provide great spectator opportunities with a fast pace, constant action and lots of corners.

Where: The race course will have a Start/Finish located on Lockey Avenue at the rear of the Capitol Building. The race will proceed from the Start/Finish Line westward to the parking lot entrance, through the parking lot to Broadway, turn left (east, wrong way in the west-bound lane) onto Broadway, turn left (north) onto Sanders left (west) onto Fifth, left (south) onto Roberts, right (west) onto Lockey back to the start area. (See Map on next page).



Streets and parking along the race course will be closed to vehicle traffic, so plan on parking near the course and walking to any of the several great viewing locations. For the safety of racers and spectators we urge all spectators to listen to the course marshals and exercise caution when crossing the course, and to please leave pets at home.

Street Closures: In order to provide the closed course, the following street closures will be needed (see map below):

  • West bound lane of Broadway Ave from Sanders St. to Wallace St.
  • Sanders St. from Broadway to 6th Avenue
  • Roberts St. from 6th Avenue to dead end between Broadway Ave. and Lockey Ave.
  • Lockey Ave. from Sanders to Montana
  • Fifth avenue from Sanders St to Roberts St


Parking Closures: Curb parking along all closed streets will be closed from 4pm until 10pm during the race. Parking lots located inside the race course boundary will be closed from 4pm to 10pm during the race.



How:

  • Organization will be spearheaded by the Great Divide Cycling Club and its 50 members.
  • Sponsorship - we will offer a $500 prize list to be provided by the entry fees and our club/race sponsors.


Other:

  • Participants - We expect approximately 80-100 participants from Montana, Idaho, and Eastern Washington. Many of the racers last year thought that this was one of the best events in Montana.
  • Spectators - We have seen crowds of 500+ people for past criteriums held in Helena.
  • Insurance - the event would be sanctioned by the United States Cycling Federation and would be covered by a $1,000,000 event liability policy. Additionally, each licensed rider caries a personal injury policy.
  • Publicity - Race brochures/posters will be sent to all riders and bike shops in the Montana, Idaho and Eastern Washington area. We will also have pre- and post-race press releases sent to the I.R. and the AP. We hope to get KTVH to cover the event. Last year we had pre and post event coverage on KTVH and in the IR and had a great center page photo on the sports page in the IR.
  • Two free kids races have been added to provide another fun event and to help draw a larger number of spectators.
